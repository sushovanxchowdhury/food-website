2.MACHER JHOL

Macher jhol is a popular Bengali fish curry that is known for its light and flavorful broth. It is a staple in Bengali cuisine and is often enjoyed with steamed rice or roti (Indian flatbread). Here's all you need to know about macher jhol:

Ingredients: - Fish: Macher jhol traditionally uses freshwater fish like Rohu, Katla, or Hilsa. However, you can use any firm white fish that is readily available to you.
- Mustard oil: Mustard oil is commonly used in Bengali cooking and adds a distinct flavor to the curry. However, you can substitute it with vegetable oil if you prefer.
- Onion: Finely chopped onions are used as a base for the curry.
- Ginger and garlic: These are key aromatics that add flavor to the dish.
- Tomatoes: Tomatoes provide a tangy and slightly sweet element to the curry.
- Green chilies: These add a spicy kick to the dish. Adjust the quantity based on your spice preference.
- Turmeric powder: It adds a vibrant yellow color to the curry.
- Cumin powder: Cumin powder adds a warm and earthy flavor.
- Coriander powder: Coriander powder enhances the overall taste of the curry.
- Salt: To taste.
- Water: Sufficient water is needed to make the broth.
- Fresh coriander leaves: These are used for garnishing the dish.

Instructions:
1. Clean the fish thoroughly and marinate it with salt and turmeric powder. Set it aside for about 15 minutes.
2. Heat mustard oil in a pan or kadai over medium heat.
3. Add chopped onions and saut√© until they turn translucent.
4. Add ginger and garlic paste to the pan and cook for a minute until the raw aroma disappears.
5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
6. Add turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt to the pan. Mix well and cook for a minute.
7. Add water to the pan and bring it to a boil.
8. Gently slide the marinated fish into the boiling broth. Be careful not to break the fish pieces.
9. Cover the pan and let the fish simmer in the broth for about 10-15 minutes or until the fish is cooked through and flavors are well combined.
10. Once the fish is cooked, remove the pan from heat and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
11. Serve macher jhol hot with steamed rice or roti.

Macher jhol is a light and comforting fish curry that showcases the delicate flavors of the fish and the aromatic spices. It is a beloved dish in Bengali households and is often enjoyed as a comforting meal. Give it a try and savor the flavors of Bengali cuisine!
