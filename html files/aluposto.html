1. ALU POSTO

Alu posto is a traditional Bengali dish that features potatoes (alu) and poppy seeds (posto) as its main ingredients.
It is a popular vegetarian dish that is known for its rich and creamy texture. Here's all you need to know about alu posto:


Ingredients:
- Potatoes: You'll need medium-sized potatoes, preferably waxy ones, that are peeled and diced.
- Poppy seeds: Poppy seeds are the star ingredient of alu posto. They are used to make a thick and flavorful paste. The seeds are usually soaked in water before grinding.
- Green chilies: These add a spicy kick to the dish. Adjust the quantity based on your spice preference.
- Mustard oil: Mustard oil is commonly used in Bengali cuisine and imparts a distinct flavor to alu posto. However, you can substitute it with vegetable oil if you prefer.
- Turmeric powder: It adds a vibrant yellow color to the dish.
- Salt: To taste.
- Sugar: A small amount of sugar is often added to balance the flavors.


Instructions:
1. Soak the poppy seeds in water for 1-2 hours, then drain the water.
2. Grind the soaked poppy seeds along with green chilies to make a smooth paste. You can use a blender or a mortar and pestle.
3. Heat mustard oil in a pan over medium heat.
4. Add the diced potatoes to the pan and fry them until they turn golden brown. Remove the potatoes from the pan and set them aside.
5. In the same pan, add a little more mustard oil if needed, then add the poppy seed paste and cook for a couple of minutes, stirring continuously.
6. Add turmeric powder, salt, and sugar to the pan and mix well.
7. Add the fried potatoes to the pan and coat them with the poppy seed mixture.
8. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes or until the potatoes are cooked through and the flavors are well combined.
9. Once the potatoes are tender, remove the pan from heat.
10. Serve alu posto hot with steamed rice or roti (Indian flatbread).
Alu posto is a comforting and flavorful dish that is loved by many. It showcases the unique use of poppy seeds in Bengali cuisine, which gives it a distinct taste. Give it a try and enjoy this classic Bengali delicacy!
